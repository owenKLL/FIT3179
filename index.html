Global Sustainability & Wealth

Scope

This relationship between sustainability and wealth will be investigated from
a continental level, where the data can be broken into:

Africa,  Asia,  Europe,  North America, Oceania, and South America.

There will also be a more granular country level of analysis when exploring this topic.

Some of the key metrics being discussed will be GDP,
GDP Growth,  Total Energy Consumption, CO2 Emissions,
 Low-Carbon Electricity,  Renewable Energy, and  Population Access to Electricity.

Biggest CO2 Emitting Countries

Looking at the data from 2000 to 2019, the trend
shows that Asian countries tend to be some of the
biggest emitters of CO2 in absolute terms, which can be
expected because of their larger populations.

Europe has been decreasing over this time period, with Germany
reducing from 830k (kt) to 660k (kt) across the time period.


Top 10 CO2 (kt) Emitting Countries per Year



Wealth & Energy

Narrowing in on the African continent depicts a strong
positive correlation between GDP and Total Energy Consumption per capita,
which can be seen by the 45 degree line formed on the scatterplot.

This is in contrast to South America, which forms a flat line
indicating a weak correlation present.

The general movement of the points indicates a general increase in GDP and
Total Energy Consumption per capita, while the increase in
pont size in the upper right quadrant suggests that increased
Renewable Energy (%) is associated with an increase in
GDP and Total Energy Consumption per capita.


GDP vs Total Energy Consumption (kWh/person) per Capita



Low Carbon Electricity

This chart displays how the Average Low-Carbon Electricity (%) of each
continent per year compares to one another in a proportional sense.

Oceania has remained relatively the same over the time period,
but we can see that Europe has increased by about 15%
while South America has decreased by about 5% to about
the same average of approximately 60% Electricity from
Low-Carbon sources.


Average Low-Carbon Electricity (%) per Year



GDP Growth during Covid-19

So far there is reasonable evidence to suggest a relationship
between sustainability efforts and wealth particularly in recent years.

However, the Covid-19 pandemic looks to have had a large
impact globally which could affect many sustainability efforts.

Of note, Guyana in South America had a major increase
in GDP by 43.48% in 2020, so it would be
interesting to see how much of this gets invested in
sustainability in the future years, given a lot of their wealth
is sourced from oil.


GDP Growth in 2020 (%)



Future Sustainability

Regarding future sustainability efforts, it appears that Africa will represent
a great opportunity for future endeavours.

As this is an area of immense population growth,
with a lesser percentage of the population having
Access to Electricity, how this continent tackles sustainability
will have a lasting impact thus representing an area for global investment.
